Capability framing

Three service pillars with real weight

These cards are intentionally not soft “feature boxes.” They read like engineered operating lanes: fewer words, larger statements, and enough breathing room to feel custom-built rather than assembled from a template kit.

01 / Executive relocations

Critical moves with zero improvisation.

Residential and corporate transitions handled through tightly sequenced planning, premium packing standards, and cleaner stakeholder communication.

Best for senior households, offices, and staged settlements
02 / Specialist freight

Difficult access. Awkward loads. Controlled outcomes.

A lane for machinery, sensitive assets, and non-standard payloads where confidence depends on crew discipline and equipment choice.

Best for oversized, fragile, or high-value handling
03 / Storage and staging

Secure holding between one site and the next.

An operational buffer for projects that need controlled timing, inventory accuracy, and a cleaner bridge between outbound and inbound delivery windows.

Best for phased projects, renovations, and settlement gaps

Lead capture direction

A serious enquiry form, not a limp contact box

This section is designed for the final Astro implementation to post into an n8n webhook. The fields are framed around operational intake, so the first submission already gives dispatch-level context instead of just name, email, and a vague message.
