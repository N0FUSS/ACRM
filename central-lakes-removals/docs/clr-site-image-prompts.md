# Central Lakes Removals Site Image Prompt Guide

This document defines the image prompts, scene direction, and usage notes for the Central Lakes Removals website image set. The goal is to keep every image aligned with the Warm Craftsmanship direction: premium, human, owner-led, practical, calm, and specific to Central Otago.

Use real photography of Russell Brown and the actual Central Lakes Removals truck wherever possible. If generated imagery is used as an interim placeholder, do not present it as a real photo of Russell or a real completed job.

## Image System Principles

Every image should feel like documentary commercial photography, not stock imagery.

The image set must communicate:

1. Russell is personally involved and accountable.
2. The team handles belongings carefully.
3. The business is based in Cromwell and connected to Central Otago.
4. The service feels premium without feeling corporate or distant.
5. The visual tone works against dark midnight backgrounds, warm cream text, muted grey copy, and restrained brass CTAs.

Avoid:

1. Stock families holding cardboard boxes.
2. Fake smiling models.
3. Generic truck-only hero images.
4. Over-edited AI portraits.
5. Cartoon moving illustrations.
6. Random city skyline imagery.
7. Cheap discount-removals styling.
8. Chaotic, unsafe, or careless moving scenes.
9. Glossy corporate poses.
10. Any generated image that includes fake logos, unreadable truck signage, warped hands, or distorted faces.

## Global Base Prompt

Use this base prompt for every image generation or photographer art-direction brief:

```text
Authentic documentary commercial photography for a premium owner-led moving company in Central Otago, New Zealand. Warm late-afternoon natural light, calm practical mood, deep midnight-compatible shadows, warm cream highlights, subtle brass warmth, slightly cinematic contrast, natural skin tones, real workwear, clean but lived-in equipment, careful professional movement, generous negative space for website copy, premium but human, no glossy AI look, no stock-photo posing, no cartoon styling, no fake smiling models, no clutter, no exaggerated drama.
```

## 1. Homepage Hero Image

Current slot: `public/images/generated/russell-hero.webp`

Primary purpose: establish the owner-led trust story in the first viewport.

Prompt:

```text
Real Russell Brown, or a reference-based owner-operator placeholder, standing beside the Central Lakes Removals truck in Cromwell or a Central Otago residential setting. He is in clean dark workwear, calm expression, practical stance, one hand resting lightly on the truck door or a folded moving blanket. The truck is visible but not overpowering. Background shows subtle Central Otago hills, dry grass, stone driveway, or a quiet rural-residential street. Warm golden side light, deep controlled shadows, premium editorial composition, 4:5 vertical crop, subject on right third with dark negative space on left for headline text.
```

Scene direction:

1. Russell should feel calm, capable, and directly accountable.
2. The truck should support the owner story, not replace it.
3. The image should have enough dark negative space to sit comfortably beside the hero headline.
4. Shoot or crop for desktop first, then verify the tablet crop still reads clearly.

Avoid:

1. A truck-only hero.
2. A forced grin or crossed-arm corporate pose.
3. Distracting signage, clutter, or bright saturated backgrounds.

Suggested alt text:

```text
Russell Brown of Central Lakes Removals standing beside the company moving truck.
```

Primary purpose: make the owner-led positioning feel human, personal, and credible.

Prompt:

```text
Close editorial portrait of Russell Brown, owner of Central Lakes Removals, standing near the rear of the moving truck or beside neatly stacked moving blankets. Calm direct eye contact, experienced and approachable expression, dark navy or charcoal workwear, natural posture, no forced smile. Background softly blurred with hints of truck body, straps, blankets, or Central Otago landscape. Warm natural light from one side, subtle vignette, high-end service business portrait, 4:5 crop, face clear and emotionally readable against a dark-compatible background.
```

Scene direction:

1. This should be more personal than the hero image.
2. Russell's expression must be readable and trustworthy.
3. Keep the frame simple, with one clear subject.
4. The background can show moving equipment, but it should stay secondary.

Avoid:

1. Selfie angles.
2. Harsh shadows over the eyes.
3. Busy background details.
4. Any pose that feels aggressive, awkward, or overly casual.

Suggested alt text:

```text
Portrait of Russell Brown, owner of Central Lakes Removals.
```

Primary purpose: ground the business in Cromwell, Central Otago, and wider South Island routes.

Prompt:

```text
Clean Central Lakes Removals moving truck parked on a quiet Cromwell or Central Otago roadside with dry hills, lake light, vineyards, stone walls, or rural residential surroundings in the background. No people required. The truck is angled three-quarter front or rear, clean but believable, not showroom sterile. Warm golden-hour light, long soft shadows, darkened sky or hillside tones compatible with midnight website palette, 16:9 landscape crop, generous negative space above or beside truck.
```

Scene direction:

1. Use this for service areas, route coverage, or trust proof.
2. Let the landscape show locality without turning into a tourism image.
3. Keep the truck clean, readable, and believable.
4. Do not use this as the primary homepage hero unless Russell is also present.

Avoid:

1. Random urban skyline.
2. Overly polished vehicle-render look.
3. Fake or distorted truck branding.
4. A location that does not resemble Central Otago.

Suggested alt text:

```text
Central Lakes Removals truck in a Central Otago landscape.
```

## 6. Protected Furniture Detail Shot

Recommended filename: `public/images/site/protected-furniture-detail.webp`

Primary purpose: add tactile proof of careful handling and premium craftsmanship.

Prompt:

```text
Close-up documentary detail of a polished timber cabinet, table edge, or upholstered chair being wrapped in a thick dark moving blanket. Hands fastening a strap carefully, texture of blanket, brass-toned buckle or warm highlight, clean practical equipment. No faces needed. Shallow depth of field, warm side light, high contrast, tactile craftsmanship feel, 16:9 or 3:2 crop.
```

Scene direction:

1. This should feel tactile and careful.
2. Texture matters: blanket weave, timber grain, strap tension, hand placement.
3. Keep it simple enough to work inside a service card or supporting section.
4. Warm highlights should echo the brass CTA colour without looking orange.

Avoid:

1. Dirty equipment.
2. Plastic-looking AI textures.
3. Overly shallow focus where the action becomes unclear.
4. Any impression that the strap is damaging the item.

Suggested alt text:

```text
Protected furniture wrapped with moving blankets and straps.
```
