# Central Lakes Removals Homepage Coding Agent Prompt

## 1. Purpose of this document

Use this document to design or build the Central Lakes Removals homepage.

It contains the homepage objective, section order, copy direction, layout logic, component requirements, mobile priorities, visual rules, conversion priorities, SEO signals, and coding agent instructions.

## 2. Homepage objective

The homepage must make this clear:

> A good move is decided before the heavy lifting starts.

The page must convert serious moving enquiries by showing that Central Lakes Removals is not just selling transport, labour, or a truck.

The value is Russell Brown’s ability to assess the job, read the risks, make the right calls on site, and take responsibility for the outcome.

Owner involvement is supporting proof.

The commercial differentiator is Russell Brown’s judgement, standards, care, lived expertise, accountability, and operating philosophy.

## 3. Repetition control rule

Do not make consecutive sections repeat the same message in different words.

Each homepage section has a different job:

1. Hero: create immediate trust through risk insight.
2. Trust proof bar: validate credibility quickly.
3. Russell difference: explain practical judgement.
4. Services overview: route visitors to the right service.
5. Reviews: prove the experience through customer evidence.
6. Process: reduce uncertainty.
7. Premium value: justify paying properly.
8. Service areas: clarify geography truthfully.
9. About Russell: deepen the human trust anchor.
10. Final quote: convert.

If a heading could fit three different sections, it is too generic.

## 4. Visual style

Use:

1. Dark midnight blue or near black background
2. Generous negative space
3. Warm off white typography
4. Muted grey supporting text
5. Brassy gold CTAs
6. Restrained brass accents
7. Real imagery of Russell
8. Strong editorial typography
9. Premium, calm layout rhythm
10. Human detail
11. Clear trust architecture

Do not create a generic moving company website.

Do not create a loud franchise style website.

Do not create a cheap, discount driven, box and truck template.

## 14. Moving process section

### Purpose

Reduce uncertainty and show the customer what happens next.

### Headline

A clear process before moving day pressure builds.

### Intro

A good move starts with the right information. The process should help customers understand what to send through, what will be reviewed, and how the job will be approached.

### Steps

#### 1. Tell us about your move

Send through your moving locations, preferred date, access details, main items, and anything unusual we should know.

#### 2. We review the practical details

The move is checked for access, timing, inventory, route, risk, and anything that may need extra planning.

#### 3. The job is planned before arrival

The team arrives with the right equipment, a clear approach, and an understanding of the move day priorities.

#### 4. Your items are handled with care

Furniture and belongings are protected, loaded, transported, and placed with attention to the details that matter.

#### 5. Loose ends are followed through

If anything needs clarification, adjustment, or follow up, you deal directly with the person responsible.

### CTA

Start Planning Your Move
