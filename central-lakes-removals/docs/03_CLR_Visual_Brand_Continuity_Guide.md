# Central Lakes Removals Visual Brand Continuity Guide

## 1. Purpose of this document

This guide controls the visual and verbal continuity of the Central Lakes Removals website.

Use it whenever creating new pages, sections, design components, images, copy, schema, SEO content, AI prompts, or coding agent instructions.

Its job is to prevent drift.

## 2. Brand essence

Central Lakes Removals should feel like a premium, human first moving service personally anchored by Russell Brown.

The visual brand must communicate:

1. Care
2. Control
3. Experience
4. Accountability
5. Human trust
6. Premium service
7. Calm confidence
8. Practical competence
9. Russell Brown’s judgement
10. Russell Brown’s standards
11. A properly planned move

## 3. Brand position

The positioning is:

> Premium moving shaped by Russell Brown’s judgement, standards, care, and accountability.

The site should make it clear that customers are not just hiring a truck.

They are hiring:

1. Russell’s judgement
2. Russell’s standards
3. Russell’s experience
4. Russell’s accountability
5. Russell’s care
6. Russell’s operating philosophy
7. A careful dedicated team
8. A properly planned move

Owner involvement is supporting proof.

It is not the whole differentiator.

1. Dark

5. Over animated

8. Bright tradie style

## 5. Colour system

### Main background

`#05080F`

Use for most page backgrounds.

Meaning:

1. Serious
2. Premium
3. Calm
4. Controlled
5. Deep and grounded

### Secondary background

`#0B111D`

Use for section variation, bands, and subtle contrast.

### Card background

`#101826`

Use for service cards, review cards, quote form panels, process cards, and supporting content blocks.

### Main text

`#F4F0E8`

Warm off white. Use for headings and important copy.

### Secondary text

`#A8AFBA`

Muted cool grey. Use for paragraphs, captions, supporting lines, and less important content.

### Border colour

`#253044`

Low contrast blue grey. Use for cards, form fields, dividers, and subtle structure.

### Primary brass

`#B8954B`

Use for primary CTAs and key conversion moments.

### Brass hover

`#C9A85A`

Use for button hover and selected link states.

### CTA text

`#06080D`

Use on brass buttons.

## 6. Brass usage rules

Brass is a conversion and value signal.

Use brass for:

1. Primary buttons
2. Important text links
3. Small eyebrow labels
4. Selected numbers
5. Thin accent lines
6. Focus states
7. Subtle hover states

Do not use brass for:

1. Large decorative backgrounds
2. Every icon
3. Every heading
4. Heavy borders everywhere
5. Unimportant labels
6. Fake luxury decoration

Rule:

> If everything is gold, nothing feels valuable.

## 7. Negative space rules

Negative space is part of the brand.

Use space to create:

1. Calm
2. Confidence
3. Premium feel
4. Readability
5. Trust
6. Clear hierarchy
7. Conversion confidence

Do not fill every area with content.

Do not stack dense cards without breathing room.

Do not reduce section spacing just to fit more information above the fold.

## 8. Typography direction

### Headings

Use a premium editorial serif or refined display font.

Heading qualities:

1. Strong
2. Legible
3. Mature
4. Premium
5. Not decorative for the sake of it

### Body text

Use a clean modern sans serif.

Body qualities:

1. Highly readable
2. Practical
3. Clear on mobile
4. Suitable for service information

### Typography rules

1. Large headings
2. Short body blocks
3. Strong line height
4. Avoid tiny text
5. Avoid dense paragraphs
6. Avoid too many font weights
7. Maintain strong contrast
8. Keep mobile reading effortless

## 9. Layout rhythm

Every section should have:

1. One core message
2. One clear visual role
3. One dominant hierarchy
4. One primary action or no action
5. Enough space to feel intentional
6. A different copy angle from the section before it

Avoid building every section as a card grid.

Avoid repeating the same brand claim through paraphrased headings. The homepage should move the visitor through a sequence: risk insight, proof, explanation, services, reviews, process, value, geography, human depth, enquiry.

The site should alternate rhythm:

1. Hero split layout
2. Proof bar
3. Editorial Russell difference section
4. Card grid
5. Review proof section
6. Process steps
7. Full width premium value statement
8. Location grid
9. Founder profile
10. Quote form

## 10. Button system

3. Strong padding
4. Slightly rounded corners

### Secondary button

Text examples:

1. Talk to Russell
2. Meet Russell
3. View Moving Services
4. Read Customer Reviews

Style:

1. Transparent background
2. Warm off white text
3. Subtle border
4. Brass border on hover
5. No competing visual weight against primary CTA

## 11. Card system

Cards must feel refined, not cheap.

Use cards for:

1. Services
2. Reviews
3. Process steps
4. Location links
5. Quote form

Card style:

1. Background `#101826`
2. Border `#253044`
3. Subtle radius
4. Low contrast depth
5. Clean spacing
6. No aggressive shadows
7. Subtle hover state only where clickable

Avoid:

1. Bright icons
2. Cartoon graphics
3. Heavy borders
4. Large shadows
5. Cramped card text
6. Too many cards on one screen

The site must use real imagery wherever possible.

Priority images:

1. Russell hero portrait
2. Russell beside truck
3. Russell working or directing on move day
4. Real truck in Central Otago context
5. Real handling details
6. Protected furniture
7. Moving blankets, straps, and equipment
8. Team moving carefully

Image style:

1. Real
2. Capable
3. Calm
4. Slightly cinematic
5. Dark compatible
6. Not over saturated
7. Not stock looking
8. Not AI glossy

Do not use:

1. Stock families with boxes
2. Fake smiling models
3. Generic truck only hero images
4. Over edited AI images of people
5. Cartoon moving illustrations
6. Random city skyline images

## 15. Page writing formula

For most pages, use this structure:

1. Clear Russell Brown trust anchor
2. Problem or customer concern
3. How Russell’s judgement and standards handle it
4. Proof or trust signal
5. Practical process
6. Location relevance if applicable
7. Clear quote CTA

## 16. Service page continuity

Each service page should include:

1. Page specific hero
2. Russell Brown trust angle
3. Service explanation
4. What is included
5. What affects planning or price
6. Common situations
7. Process section
8. Proof or review block
9. Service area relevance
10. Quote CTA

Service pages must not become thin SEO pages.

Each page must help a real customer make a decision.

## 17. Location page continuity

Each location page should include:

1. Clear service area language
2. Cromwell base disclosure
3. Route or regional relevance
4. Local moving context
5. Services available to that area
6. Russell Brown trust message
7. Proof and review support
8. Quote CTA

Correct language:

> Central Lakes Removals is based in Cromwell and provides moving services involving Queenstown.

Incorrect language:

> Central Lakes Removals is a Queenstown based moving company.

Only use the second version if it becomes factually true.

## 18. Review presentation rules

Reviews should be used as proof, not decoration.

Best review themes:

1. Careful handling
2. Clear communication
3. Hard working team
4. Calm problem solving
5. Professional and trustworthy
6. Personal accountability
7. Difficult move handled well

Rules:

1. Use real review excerpts.
2. Preserve customer meaning.
3. Do not invent names.
4. Do not over polish review language.
5. Do not hide all reviews inside a third party widget.
6. Use structured review proof near the top half of important pages.
7. Do not imply review claims that are not visible or verifiable.

## 19. Form continuity

Forms should feel calm and premium.

Quote forms should ask for:

1. Name
2. Phone
3. Email
4. Moving from
5. Moving to
6. Preferred moving date
7. Type of move
8. Main items or inventory
9. Access notes
10. Packing help required
11. Message

Form tone:

> The more detail you provide, the easier it is to plan your move accurately.

Reassurance:

> No pressure. No generic estimate. Just a careful look at what your move actually needs.

## 20. Accessibility and usability rules

1. Maintain strong text contrast.
2. Make buttons large enough for mobile thumbs.
3. Do not rely on colour alone for meaning.
4. Use readable font sizes.
5. Add descriptive alt text for real images.
6. Keep forms clear and labelled.
7. Use semantic headings.
8. Avoid motion that distracts or harms usability.
9. Keep mobile navigation simple.
10. Make phone and quote actions easy to find.
11. Keep focus states visible.
12. Avoid text over busy image areas unless contrast is controlled.

## 21. Performance rules

1. Compress images.
2. Use modern image formats where suitable.
3. Avoid unnecessary JavaScript.
4. Avoid bloated animation libraries unless justified.
5. Keep above the fold fast.
6. Lazy load non critical images.
7. Use clean semantic markup.
8. Do not let visual polish damage page speed.
9. Do not let typewriter effects delay the hero message.
10. Keep LCP image predictable and optimised.

## 22. SEO and AIO continuity rules

1. Make Central Lakes Removals clear as the business entity.
2. Make Russell Brown clear as the person entity.
3. Make Cromwell clear as the base.
4. Make service areas clear without false branch claims.
5. Use real services with clear page intent.
6. Use structured headings.
7. Use descriptive internal links.
8. Keep page copy useful to humans, not just search engines.
9. Avoid location stuffing.
10. Keep all claims aligned with visible content and businessConfig.

## 23. Continuity checklist

Before publishing any new page or component, check:

1. Is Russell Brown still central to the trust story?
2. Is the page clearly premium, human, calm, and accountable?
3. Is the commercial differentiator Russell’s judgement, standards, care, lived expertise, and accountability?
4. Is owner involvement used as proof rather than the whole message?
5. Is the design dark, spacious, calm, and restrained?
6. Are brass CTAs used sparingly and effectively?
7. Is Cromwell identified correctly as the base?
8. Are service areas described truthfully?
9. Are claims factual and verifiable?
10. Is the copy specific rather than generic?
11. Does the page have a clear conversion action?
12. Does the page feel like Central Lakes Removals, not a template?
13. Is the page mobile friendly?
14. Is the page fast enough to protect trust and conversion?
15. Would a serious customer feel safer after reading it?

## 24. Agent branch and code safety rules

When working in the GitHub repository:

1. Treat `main` as the source of truth unless instructed otherwise.
2. Do not merge old agent branches without reviewing deletions.
3. Do not delete the `central-lakes-removals` app tree.
4. Do not replace the production app with imported baseline folders.
5. Do not remove business config, schema, quote form, test, or CI files without explicit instruction.
6. Preserve verified claims in `business-config.ts`.
7. Do not invent phone numbers, emails, reviews, ratings, or locations.
8. Run lint, build, E2E, and Lighthouse checks before claiming production readiness.

## 25. Master continuity prompt

Use this prompt whenever an agent is creating or editing design or copy:

```text
Preserve the Central Lakes Removals visual and brand system.

Central Lakes Removals is a premium moving service based in Cromwell and serving Central Otago and wider South Island routes.

The commercial differentiator is Russell Brown’s personal standard, judgement, care, lived expertise, accountability, and operating philosophy. The company is the delivery system built around those standards.

Owner involvement is supporting proof, not the whole differentiator.

The design must use a dark midnight blue or near black background, generous negative space, warm off white text, muted grey supporting text, and restrained brassy gold CTAs.

The tone must be calm, premium, human, practical, accountable, and locally grounded.

Russell Brown must remain the trust anchor.

Do not make the page generic, cheap, loud, cluttered, over animated, stock photo driven, discount focused, or falsely located.

Do not invent claims, phone numbers, locations, or reviews.

Use brass only for meaningful conversion actions and subtle accents.

Keep the page clear, mobile friendly, fast, truthful, conversion focused, and useful to real customers.
```
